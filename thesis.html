The thesis compares ways of representing bolted joints in structural FE models — from analytically derived 6-DOF spring (CBUSH) elements to more expensive representations — and evaluates how the modelling choice propagates into random-vibration fatigue-life predictions using spectral methods. The reference joint is two SS304 plates clamped by an M10 × 1.5 grade 10.9 socket-head cap screw, excited by a NASA GEVS-type acceleration spectrum. The three tools below reproduce the thesis' analytical chain: bolt & member stiffness → load sharing → spectral fatigue life. All defaults reproduce the (corrected) thesis values.

Numbers follow the corrected scripts (ISO 68-1 minor diameter d₃ = 8.160 mm, WP on the stress-section diameter, Bickford Q per SAND2008-0371 Eqs. 13–17).

1 · Bolted-joint stiffness — VDI 2230 vs. Sandia

The bolt is condensed into a 6-DOF spring using VDI 2230 segment resiliences ($\delta_S=\sum L_i/(E A_i)$, $\beta_S=\sum L_i/(E I_i)$); the clamped members are estimated three ways: the VDI 2230 pressure cone, the Sandia Dimensionless Material Property (DMP) correlation $k_m = E_{eff}\, d_b\,(0.9991\,x_G + 0.2189\,n + 0.5234)$, and Bickford's Q-factor. 2 · Load sharing — why the member-stiffness method matters

Only the fraction $\Phi$ of an external axial load goes through the bolt; the rest unloads the clamped members. The joint opens when the clamp force $F_K = F_M - (1-\Phi)F_A$ reaches zero, i.e. at $F_{A,open} = F_M/(1-\Phi)$. Because the three member-stiffness methods disagree on $\Phi$ (DMP 0.144, VDI 0.118, Bickford 0.238 for the thesis joint), they disagree on both the alternating bolt stress and the predicted opening load.

3 · Random-vibration fatigue — spectral methods

A stress response PSD is generated by passing a NASA GEVS-type base-acceleration spectrum through a single-mode resonator with natural frequency $f_n$, damping $\zeta$ and a stress sensitivity $\sigma/g$. From the spectral moments $m_n=\int f^{\,n} S(f)\,df$ the up-crossing rate is $\nu_0=\sqrt{m_2/m_0}$, the peak rate $\nu_p=\sqrt{m_4/m_2}$ and the irregularity factor $\gamma = m_2/\sqrt{m_0 m_4}$. Fatigue life follows from Miner's rule with the S–N curve $N_f = C\,S_a^{-m}$ (stress-amplitude convention, $S_a=\sqrt{m_0}\,Z$), comparing Dirlik, narrowband (Rice) and Steinberg's three-band estimates — the same estimators benchmarked in the thesis.
